Getting Started

How does Device Tracker work?

Device Tracker uses your iPhone's Bluetooth hardware to detect nearby Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) devices. After a scan it displays all discovered devices with an estimated distance and signal strength. You can save devices to "My Devices" for quick re-scanning, and set up notifications to alert you when a saved device comes into or goes out of range.

First-time setup

  1. Open Device Tracker and tap the large Bluetooth button on the main screen to start a scan.
  2. When prompted, tap Allow to grant Bluetooth access — the app cannot function without it.
  3. After the scan completes, all nearby BLE devices appear in the list.
  4. Tap a device to open its detail view (premium feature), then tap Found it to open its settings.
  5. Toggle Add to My Devices to save it for future scans.

Frequently Asked Questions

A few things to check:

  • Bluetooth permission: Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Bluetooth and make sure Device Tracker is enabled.
  • Bluetooth is on: Open Control Centre and confirm Bluetooth is active (not just "nearby" mode).
  • Real device required: Bluetooth scanning does not work in the iOS Simulator — always test on a physical iPhone.
  • Device must be advertising: Not all Bluetooth devices broadcast continuously. Keyboards, headphones, and speakers typically advertise only when in pairing mode or actively connected.
  • Move closer: Try scanning within 5–10 metres of the target device.

  • Walls, furniture, and other physical obstructions
  • Interference from other wireless devices (Wi-Fi, microwave ovens, etc.)
  • Differences in Bluetooth transmit power between device models
  • The orientation and position of both phones

For best results use the real-time signal strength percentage and the animated indicator rather than the numerical distance alone — move steadily in one direction and watch whether the signal increases or decreases.

Check each of the following in order:

  • Notification permission: Go to Settings → Notifications → Device Tracker and ensure notifications are Allowed with banners and sounds enabled.
  • Per-device toggles: Open the app, navigate to the device's settings (tap the device → Found it), and confirm that Notify when Found or Notify when Lost is switched on.
  • Device must be saved: Notifications only fire for devices added to "My Devices."
  • Background app refresh: Go to Settings → General → Background App Refresh and make sure Device Tracker is enabled.
  • Focus / Do Not Disturb: Check that an active Focus mode is not silencing notifications from Device Tracker.
